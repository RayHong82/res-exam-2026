# PAPER 1 — Legal & Land-Law Foundations (CU1–CU2)

## 1. CEA — Role & Powers
- Established **22 Oct 2010**; statutory board under **MND**; administers the **Estate Agents Act 2010 (EAA)**. Licensing regime started **1 Jan 2011**.
- Functions: license EAs, register salespersons, regulate conduct, administer **CEPCC** & **COPEA**, consumer education, investigations/discipline, administer exams & CPD.
- **3 strategic thrusts:** Effective Regulation, Industry Development, Consumer Education.

## 2. Estate Agents Act 2010 & 2025 Update
- Principal statute = EAA 2010. Subsidiary: Estate Agents (Estate Agency Work) Regulations 2010 (prescribes CEPCC & COPEA Second Schedule); Estate Agents (Dispute Resolution Schemes) Regulations 2011.
- **AML and Other Matters (Estate Agents and Developers) Act 2025:** passed 8 Apr 2025, **operational 1 Jul 2025**; strengthens AML/CFT, raised max penalties.

## 3. EA vs RES vs KEO
- **EA** = the firm — **licensed**. **RES** = the individual — **registered**.
- **KEO eligibility:** ≥21yr; ≥3yr as salesperson; ≥30 transactions in past 3yr (≥10 private, ≥10 HDB); 4 GCE 'O' + valid REA exam (within 2yr); fit-and-proper; cannot concurrently be RES/sole proprietor/director of another EA.
- **RES eligibility:** ≥21yr; 4 'O' Levels (or WPLN L5+); pass RES exam. **Single-agency rule** (only 1 EA). No moneylending/moonlighting.
- **RES fees:** application **$60**; registration **$280** (≤30 Jun) / **$140** (after 30 Jun).

## 4. CEPCC (Code of Ethics & Professional Client Care)
- Prescribed under EAW Regulations 2010; standard of conduct toward clients/public. **12 areas** incl. legal compliance, due diligence, duties to clients/public, document execution, prescribed agreements, offer/counter-offer, translation, advertising, **conflict-of-interest avoidance**, referral to professional advice, confidentiality. Breach → disciplinary action.

## 5. COPEA (Code of Practice for Estate Agents)
- Second Schedule, EAW Regulations 2010; firm-level. **4 areas:** management & supervision, Estate Agent Card, claims/complaints procedures, document & records retention.
- **Record retention ≥5 years** (Para 7(1); raised from 3 to align with AML/CFT). Electronic OK.

## 6. Conflict of Interest & Dual Representation
- **Dual representation is an OFFENCE** — one RES collecting/sharing fees from **both** sides of the same transaction. (Real case: $9,000 fine.)
- Two RES from the **same EA** on opposite sides: **not** dual rep **provided** neither collects from both sides; both must **declare in writing** they are from the same EA + obtain **written consent**.
- Overriding fees must be disclosed in writing as a potential conflict.

## 7. CPD (Continuing Professional Development)
- **2025 (outgoing):** 6 credits, Oct–Sep cycle.
- **2026 (eff. 1 Jan 2026, Project ADEPT — HIGHLY EXAMINABLE):** **16 training hours/year = 12h Structured Learning (incl. ≥4h Prescribed Essentials) + 4h Self-Directed Learning.** Cycle permanently **Jan–Dec**. **2026 PE topic: PMLPFTF (anti-money laundering).** New RES exempt in 1st cycle. CPD = condition of annual registration renewal.

## 8. Disciplinary Process & Penalties
- **Letter of Censure (LOC) framework** (less serious, non-criminal): max **$5,000 per case** (general) / **per breach** (AML, on/after 1 Jul 2025). Ladder: LOW → LOC → DC.
- **Disciplinary Committee (DC):** ≥3 from CEA Disciplinary Panel. Max penalty: **RES $100,000**, **EA $200,000** (per case for other breaches; per breach for AML). Powers: revoke/suspend, vary conditions, financial penalty, reprimand.
- **Process:** Statement of Case → respondent Admission/Defence (**14 days**) → Reply (14d) → Rejoinder (14d) → hearing → sentencing. **Appeal to MND Appeals Board within 14 days.**

## 9. Complaints & Dispute Resolution
- Complaint to CEA: hotline **1800-643-2555**. CEA handles discipline, **does NOT recover money** for consumers.
- EA must first meet complainant for amicable resolution (COPEA).
- **CEA Dispute Resolution Scheme** (Regs 2011): **Mediation** (via Singapore Mediation Centre) + **Adjudication/arbitration**.
- **Small Claims Tribunals (SCT):** up to **$20,000** (up to **$30,000** if both parties sign a **Memorandum of Consent**).

## 10. Estates & Interests in Land
- **Fee Simple** = highest freehold, indefinite, unconditional, inheritable. **Estate in Perpetuity** = freehold but **conditional** under State Lands Act. **Life Estate** = lifetime only, reverts on death.
- Leasehold = fixed duration: 99yr, 999yr (≈freehold). HDB flats = **99-yr leasehold**; HDB/JTC also grant 30/60/99yr. On expiry reverts to State.

## 11. Legal vs Equitable Interest
- **Legal** = enforceable against the whole world ("in rem"); vests on registration for Torrens land.
- **Equitable** = beneficial; enforceable against all **EXCEPT a bona fide purchaser for value without notice**.
- **Trust:** trustee holds legal title, beneficiary holds equitable interest. Resulting trust → beneficial interest follows financial contributions unless common intention proven.
- Buyer acquires equitable interest on exercising OTP/signing S&P (basis for lodging caveat).

## 12. Co-ownership: Joint Tenancy vs Tenancy-in-Common
- **JT:** own the whole jointly, NO distinct shares. **4 unities — Possession, Interest, Title, Time (PITT).** **Right of survivorship (jus accrescendi)** — interest passes automatically to survivors, NOT by will/intestacy.
- **TIC:** distinct undivided shares (may be unequal); only **unity of possession**; **NO survivorship** — share passes by will/intestacy.
- **Severance (JT→TIC):** alienation/dealing with own share; mutual agreement; mutual conduct; unilateral declaration/deed; court order.

## 13. Land Titles Act 1993 / Torrens System
- Torrens system, administered by **SLA** (Land Titles Registry). Almost all private land now under LTA.
- Principles: **Mirror** (register reflects title), **Curtain** (no need to look behind register), **Insurance** (State-guaranteed).
- **s46 LTA: indefeasibility** of registered proprietor's title — NOT absolute; exceptions = **fraud/forgery** by proprietor/agent, statutory/overriding interests. Folio = conclusive evidence. Registration vests legal title.

## 14. Registered vs Unregistered Land
- **Registered (Torrens):** register conclusive, indefeasible title, takes effect on registration.
- **Unregistered (deeds system, Registration of Deeds Act):** chain of title deeds, no State guarantee, priority by order of registration/notice. Only tiny residue remains.

## 15. Caveats
- Statutory notice/"freeze" lodged with SLA by anyone with a **caveatable interest** (exercised OTP, S&P, mortgage, lease, easement; buyer, mortgagee, CPF Board, tenant). Via **STARS** portal; fee ≈ **S$64.45**.
- Key sections: s115 lodge, s119 effect, s121 lapsing, s122 extension, s126 withdrawal, s127 lapsing on notice, s128 compensation for wrongful caveat.
- Valid **5 years**, extendable 5 more. On inconsistent dealing, SLA serves notice → caveator must get court order within **30 days** (s127). **Registrar cannot remove on own motion.** Wrongful caveat → liable in damages (s128). **Caveat is precautionary, NOT mandatory.**

## 16. Easements
- Right to use another's land for a limited purpose (right of way, drainage, services). Needs **dominant tenement** (benefits) + **servient tenement** (burdened). Registered easement noted on folio, binds successors. Is a caveatable interest & encumbrance.

## 17. Encumbrances / Mortgages / Charges
- Encumbrance = third-party right burdening land (mortgage, charge, easement, lease, lien, caveat, restrictive covenant).
- **Torrens mortgage: does NOT transfer ownership** — operates as registered charge; mortgagor retains title. Effective only on **registration**. On default, mortgagee has statutory power of sale. Folio conclusive of encumbrance.

## 18. En Bloc / Collective Sale (Land Titles (Strata) Act 1967, s84A)
- Consent (by **both** share value **and** strata floor area): **<10yr old → 90%**; **≥10yr → 80%**. Age from latest **TOP** (or CSC if no TOP).
- Process: form **Collective Sale Committee** at EGM → independent valuation → **Collective Sale Agreement** (consent within **12 months** of first signature) → **5-day cooling-off** to rescind → public tender → application to **Strata Titles Board (STB)** if not unanimous.
- **STB** mediates/adjudicates: must be satisfied sale is in **good faith** and no objector suffers **financial loss**. Objection grounds: financial loss; lack of good faith (assessed by sale price, distribution method, purchaser-SP relationship). Typical timeline 18–24 months.

## 19. Foreign Ownership — Residential Property Act 1976 (SLA/LDAU)
- **"Foreign person" includes SPRs**, foreign companies/entities.
- **NON-restricted (no approval):** condo unit; private flat/apartment; **strata landed WITHIN approved condo**; leasehold landed ≤**7 years**; commercial shophouses; industrial/commercial property; registered hotels; fully privatised ECs (≥10yr), HDB flats/shophouses (per HDB rules).
- **RESTRICTED (LDAU approval):** vacant residential land; terrace/semi-D/bungalow (landed); strata landed **outside** condo; **Sentosa Cove** landed; residential-use shophouses; serviced apartments/dormitories; mixed commercial-residential.
- **LDAU approval criteria:** PR ≥**5 years** + exceptional economic contribution. Fee **S$1,220/property**; ~30 working days.
- **Conditions on approved restricted purchase:** mainland land area ≤**15,000 sq ft**; Sentosa Cove ≤**1,800 sqm**; no disposal within **5 years**; owner-occupation only (no rental); no subdivision.
- **EC foreigner timeline:** Yr 0–5 (MOP) SC/SPR only; Yr 5–10 SC/SPR resale only; **after 10yr (privatised) → anyone incl. foreigners.**

## 20. Condo vs Landed; Tenure
- **Condo:** strata title, share of common property, **MCST** under **BMSMA**, monthly maintenance fees, shared facilities, non-restricted for foreigners.
- **Landed:** individual land title, owner controls land, no MCST, restricted for foreigners.
- **Strata landed/cluster:** hybrid with MCST; within approved condo = non-restricted, outside = restricted.

## 21. URA Master Plan, Zoning, Plot Ratio
- **Master Plan** = statutory land-use plan, reviewed ~every **5 years**, guides 10–15yr development; sets zoning + intensity (GPR + height). Draft Master Plan 2025 exhibited Nov 2024. Below the broad **Concept Plan** (~every 10yr).
- **GPR = Gross Floor Area ÷ Site Area** → max development intensity; higher GPR = higher value/en-bloc potential.
- **Change of use** requires URA planning permission.

## 22. Commercial & Industrial Property
- **Commercial:** foreigners buy freely; **9% GST**; zoned Commercial/White/mixed.
- **Industrial zoning:** **B1 (light, 50m buffer)**, **B2 (heavy, 100m buffer)**, Business Park. **60:40 rule** — ≥60% GFA core industrial use, ≤40% ancillary. **No residential use.** Foreigners buy freely; **9% GST**.
- **JTC:** leases typically **30+30 years**; subletting needs prior written JTC approval.

# PAPER 2 — Practice, Regulation & Transactions (CU3–CU4)

## 1. Estate Agency Agreements (8 prescribed forms)
| Form | Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Non-exclusive | Selling residential |
| 2 | Non-exclusive | Buying residential |
| 3 | Non-exclusive | Leasing — landlord |
| 4 | Non-exclusive | Leasing — tenant |
| 5 | Exclusive | Selling residential |
| 6 | Exclusive | Buying residential |
| 7 | Exclusive | Leasing — landlord |
| 8 | Exclusive | Leasing — tenant |

- **Exclusive:** one EA/RES only; client liable for commission even if deal closes via another/self during validity. **Max validity 3 months**, no auto-renew. **Post-expiry tail:** commission still payable if sold within **3 months** after expiry to a buyer the agent introduced during the validity period.
- **Non-exclusive (open):** multiple agencies; pay only the one who closes.
- RES must declare any conflict of interest.

## 2. Commission
- **Not fixed/regulated by CEA — fully negotiable**, documented in EAA.
- **Dual representation prohibited (offence)** — collect only from the party you represent.
- Market norms: residential sale ~2% from seller (+buyer may pay ~1%); rentals ~1 month rent from landlord (≥1yr lease).
- **SEAA Best Practice Guide for Co-Broking Commission** (eff. 1 Jul 2024): each side pays its own agent; **not a regulation** (no penalty). ~90% of RESs covered as of Feb 2025.

## 3. Co-Broking
- Most common arrangement: seller's/landlord's RES co-operates with buyer's/tenant's RES. Sale split commonly 1%+1%; rental split ½ month each (varies). SEAA guide pushes "each side pays own agent" to reduce disputes.

## 4. Advertising Rules (CEA)
- Every ad (print/online/mobile) must show: **RES name, CEA Registration No., contact number** (and EA Licence No. for the agency).
- Material info must be accurate/verified (lease term, floor area); no false/misleading/discriminatory content; need **owner's prior consent** before advertising.
- **Penalty: up to $75,000**, suspension, or revocation.

## 5. AML/CFT Obligations
- **CDD before any agreement:** obtain & document client info + written acknowledgement; verify identity via reliable independent sources; screen against **UNSC/UN Act** designated lists; check **PEP** status; apply **EDD** for higher risk.
- **STR:** file Suspicious Transaction Report to **STRO via SONAR** (legal obligation).
- **Records: keep ≥5 years.**
- Penalties: LOC up to **$5,000/breach**; DC up to **$200,000/breach (EA)** / **$100,000/breach (RES)**.

## 6. Option to Purchase (OTP)
- **HDB resale:** Option Fee **$1–$1,000**; **Option Fee + Exercise Fee ≤ $5,000** combined; Option Period **21 calendar days**, expires **4pm** last day. Both fees form part of resale price.
- **Private resale (negotiable conventions):** Option Fee ~**1%**; Exercise Fee ~**4%** (5% total deposit); Option Period ~**14 days**; completion ~**10–12 weeks**.

## 7. Sale & Purchase Agreement (SPA)
- New launch/developer: prescribed SPA. Private resale: exercised OTP often serves as binding contract. Governs loan, stamp duty (BSD/ABSD), completion date, handover.

## 8. Conveyancing & Completion
1. Sign OTP, appoint conveyancing lawyer.
2. **Lodge caveat** (buyer's interest).
3. Pre-completion: legal **requisitions** to govt bodies (PUB, LTA, NEA, IRAS, etc.) confirming no development/acquisition plans + **good root of title**; examine Certificate of Title.
4. Completion: exchange cashier's orders, keys, title, transfer instrument.
- **HDB resale:** completion ~8 weeks after HDB acceptance. If HDB is solicitor, parties attend in person at HDB Hub; private solicitor's rep may attend on behalf.

## 9. Tenancy / Lease Agreements
- **Letter of Intent (LOI)** with good-faith deposit → **Tenancy Agreement (TA)**. Key clauses: rent, duration, deposits, diplomatic clause (≥2yr leases), minor repair clause, reinstatement, "good and tenantable repair".

## 10. Lease Stamp Duty
- ≤4yr: **0.4% of total rent**. >4yr/indefinite: **0.4% × 4 × AAR**. **AAR ≤$1,000 exempt**; min $1; rounded down. Pay within **14 days** (local) / **30 days** (overseas). Tenant pays by convention; both jointly liable.

## 11. Security Deposit Norms
- Convention: **1 month rent per lease year** (1mo for 1yr, 2mo for 2yr). Separate utilities deposit common. Returned ~7–30 days after lease end. Covers tenant damage, **NOT fair wear and tear**.

## 12. Landlord vs Tenant Obligations
- **Tenant:** keep premises in "good and tenantable repair" (fair wear & tear excepted); pay rent, utilities, lease stamp duty.
- **Minor repair clause:** tenant bears repair up to ~**$150–$300/item**; above = landlord.
- **Landlord:** major/structural repairs, fair wear & tear, return deposit. **Fair wear and tear must NOT be deducted.**

## 13. HDB Eligibility Schemes
- **Public** (≥1 SC + family nucleus); **Fiancé/Fiancée** (register marriage within 3mo of key collection); **Single SC** (≥35yr); **Joint Singles** (2–4 singles, all ≥35yr SC); **Non-Citizen Spouse/Family**; **Orphans**; **Conversion**. *(Govt review of singles age 35 & BTO ceilings announced Aug 2025 but not yet in effect — use age 35.)*

## 14. CPF Housing Grants
- **EHG:** families up to **$120,000**, singles up to **$60,000**; ceiling $9,000/$4,500; needs 12 continuous months employment; BTO & resale. Replaces AHG + Special Grant.
- **Family Grant (resale only):** $80,000 (2–4rm) / $50,000 (5rm+); ceiling $14,000. Singles Grant $40,000 / $25,000.
- **Proximity Housing Grant (resale only, no ceiling):** live WITH parents/child $30,000 (single $15,000); live NEAR (≤4km) $20,000 (single $10,000).
- **Max combined family resale grants ≈ $230,000** ($80k+$120k+$30k).

## 15. Ethnic Integration Policy (EIP) & SPR Quota
- Applies to all HDB flats, NOT private. EIP max: **Chinese 84%/87%**, **Malay 22%/25%**, **Indian/Other 12%/15%** (neighbourhood/block). **SPR quota 5%/8%**; **Malaysians exempt**. When quota full, seller of that group can't sell to same-group buyer (except buyer & seller same group). Updated 1st of each month.

## 16. Renting Out Flat/Bedroom
- **Whole flat:** only **SC** owners, after MOP, HDB approval, min 6mo; max tenancy 3yr (SC/Malaysian) / 2yr (other non-citizens); **Non-Citizen Quota 8%/11%** (Malaysians exempt). Prime: no whole-flat rental even after MOP; Plus: restricted.
- **Bedrooms:** 3rm+ only (3rm max 1 room; 4rm+ max 2 rooms); SC or SPR owner, can rent before MOP if owner still lives there. Min 6mo.
- **Occupancy cap (relaxed 22 Jan 2024–31 Dec 2026):** **8** for 4rm+, 6 for 3rm, 4 for 1-/2-rm.

## 17. HDB Resale Procedure
1. Register Intent to Sell/Buy on HDB Resale Portal (HFE check); 7-day seller cooling-off.
2. Grant OTP: Option Fee ≤$1,000; option period 21 days.
3. Exercise: Option + Exercise Fee ≤ $5,000.
4. Request for Value (buyer; $120; ~10 working days; valid 3mo).
5. Submit resale application (admin fee $40 for 1–2rm / $80 for 3rm+, each side).
6. HDB processing ~2–4 weeks; completion ~8 weeks after acceptance. Total OTP→completion ≈ 8–12 weeks.

# Common Exam Traps (build distractors around these)
1. **BSD is buyer-profile-independent** (same for all); only **ABSD** depends on citizenship + property count.
2. **BSD/ABSD/SSD on the HIGHER of price or market value** — not the lower.
3. **ABSD FTA exemption** — US + Iceland/Liechtenstein/Norway/Switzerland nationals/PRs treated as SC.
4. **JT vs TIC** — survivorship (JT, auto to survivor) vs willable share (TIC); don't swap.
5. **Plot ratio (floor area) vs height limit** — distinct controls.
6. **Equitable right defeated by bona fide purchaser** for value without notice — legal right survives against all.
7. **Foreigners CANNOT buy landed/GCB/vacant land without LDAU approval**; CAN buy condo units.
8. **Caveat is precautionary, NOT mandatory**; Registrar can't remove on own motion.
9. **SSD residential holding period now 4 years** (purchases on/after 4 Jul 2025), rate 16/12/8/4%.
10. **"EXCEPT / NOT" inversions** — read the question stem carefully.
11. **MSR (30%) applies only to HDB & developer EC**, not private resale.
12. **HDB has no CPF Withdrawal Limit** (120% WL = private only).
13. **No income ceiling to buy resale outright** — ceiling only for grants/new flats.
