## Paper 1

### Section A (Q1–60, 各1分)

**Q1. 答案 A**  _(easy)_ — CEA was established on 22 Oct 2010 as a statutory board under the Ministry of National Development (MND) and administers the Estate Agents Act 2010, licensing EAs and registering RESs. B is wrong: CEA is not under MAS and does NOT fix commission (commission is fully negotiable). C is wrong: CEA is separate from HDB and does not approve resale transactions. D is wrong: CEA is a statutory regulator with full statutory disciplinary powers, not a self-regulatory trade body. 中文一句话: CEA 是国家发展部下的法定机构，依《地产代理法令》监管行业。  _[CEA – About CEA / Estate Agents Act 2010]_

**Q2. 答案 D**  _(easy)_ — The CEPCC is prescribed under the Estate Agents (Estate Agency Work) Regulations 2010 and sets the conduct standard owed by EAs and RESs to clients and the public; breach leads to disciplinary action. B is wrong because the CEPCC sits in subsidiary regulations, not the Act's main body, and binds all RES/EAs, not only KEOs. C is wrong - that is land law, unrelated. A confuses CEPCC with the Dispute Resolution Schemes Regulations. 中文：CEPCC 由《地产代理(地产代理工作)条例 2010》规定，约束 EA 与 RES 对客户及公众的行为。  _[EA (Estate Agency Work) Regulations 2010 (CEPCC); CEA]_

**Q3. 答案 C**  _(easy)_ — Under the Estate Agents Act 2010, the firm (EA) is LICENSED while the individual salesperson (RES) is REGISTERED, and the RES must be registered through a licensed EA. The single-agency rule limits an RES to exactly one EA at any time. A is wrong: an RES is registered (not 'licensed') and cannot work independently outside an EA. B is wrong: an RES does not need an EA licence (that is the firm-level licence). D is wrong: the single-agency rule prohibits registering with two EAs concurrently. 中文一句：行号(EA)是被'发牌'，经纪(RES)是通过一家持牌行号'注册'，且只能挂靠一家(single-agency rule)。  _[CEA – Estate Agents Act 2010; single-agency rule]_

**Q4. 答案 C**  _(easy)_ — Fee simple is the highest and most complete form of freehold ownership: indefinite in duration, unconditional, and inheritable. B describes an Estate in Perpetuity, which is freehold but conditional under the State Lands Act. A is wrong because 999-year is a leasehold (approximating freehold) but is still a fixed term, not fee simple. D describes a life estate, which ends on the holder's death. 中文: 永久产权（fee simple）是最高、无条件、可继承且无限期的产权；B是有条件的永久地契，C是租赁地契，D是终身产权。  _[FACT PACK P1.10 Estates & Interests in Land]_

**Q5. 答案 B**  _(easy)_ — The Curtain principle means a prospective buyer can rely on the register as it stands and does not need to investigate the chain of past dealings hidden 'behind the curtain'. Option A describes the Insurance principle (State guarantee/compensation). Option C describes the Mirror principle (register reflects the title accurately). Option D is a general effect of registration vesting legal title, not one of the three named principles. 中文: 'Curtain'(帷幕)原则指买方无需查看登记簿背后的历史交易;A是保险原则,C是镜子原则,D只是登记的法律效果。  _[Land Titles Act 1993 (Torrens principles: Mirror, Curtain, Insurance)]_

**Q6. 答案 A**  _(easy)_ — The Family Grant (CPF Housing Grant for resale flats, Families) has an income ceiling of $14,000. At $11,500 the couple is within that ceiling and can potentially qualify. B is wrong because they are a married couple, not single applicants. **Q10. 答案 D**  _(easy)_ — For a first housing loan from a financial institution, the maximum LTV is 75% PROVIDED the tenure is ≤30 years AND the loan does not extend beyond the borrower's age 65 — both conditions are met here. 55% applies only when those conditions are NOT met (tenure >30yr or extends beyond age 65). 65% is not an LTV tier (a distractor). 80% was the old HDB concessionary loan limit, cut to 75% on 20 Aug 2024, and never applied to bank loans. 中文：首贷且年限≤30年、不超65岁，最高LTV为75%。  _[MAS Rules for New Housing Loans (LTV limits)]_

**Q11. 答案 C**  _(easy)_ — BSD is buyer-profile-independent: it is computed solely on the price or market value (whichever higher) and does not vary by citizenship or number of properties owned. **Q16. 答案 D**  _(easy)_ — In a joint tenancy the right of survivorship (jus accrescendi) operates: on the death of one joint tenant, their interest passes automatically to the surviving joint tenant(s) and CANNOT be disposed of by will or intestacy.
